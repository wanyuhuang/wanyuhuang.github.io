About me

I am currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Civil Engineering, Johns Hopkins University, advised by Takeru Igusa. Before joining Johns Hopkins University, I obtained my M.S. degree in Industrial Engineering from Iowa State University and B.E. degree in Automation from Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

Research

  • My research interests lie in the field of systems approaches for simulating human's behaviors and interactions.
  • My current research focuses include: 1) systems dynamics and agent-based model for smoking cessation, 2) machine learning for studying children's health.

Projects

  • Promoting Evidence-Based Tobacco Smoking Cessation Treatment in Community Mental Health Clinics
    The Johns Hopkins ALACRITY Center for Health and Longevity in Mental Health
    Director: Gail Daumit. Associate Director: Beth McGinty.
    The Center uses cutting-edge transdisciplinary methods to advance short- and long-term strategies to reduce cardiovascular risk and premature mortality in the population with serious mental illness. We are working on systems models that can be used to estimate the effects of various interventions to improve physical health among people with serious mental illness on long-term public health outcomes.
  • Development of Expansion Planning Methods and Tools for Handling Uncertainty
    PSERC M-37
    PI: Lizhi Wang. Co-PI: James McCalley and Christopher DeMarco.
    We are working to develop a new method for generating a small number of high quality scenarios to help existing expansion planning models and tools to produce more resilient solutions. The effectiveness of the new method will be validated using existing planning tools on realistic case studies.
  • PIIR DDSI Big Data for Sustainable Cities Decision Making
    Sustainable Cities project
    PI: Ulrike Passe.
    We developed an integreted agent-based model for model residents decisions for adopting energy-saving practices. We also explored what factors matter when residents make these decisions.
